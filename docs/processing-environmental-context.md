# Processing environmental context (Phase 5C)

## What is claimed, and what is not

Environmental content is **reference-level and evidence-backed only**. The two
standing prohibitions:

1. **No sustainability scores.** None are computed, implied, or displayed.
2. **No "greener than" claim** without comparable evidence. Where a genuine
   trade-off exists it is stated _as a trade-off_ and left unresolved, because
   resolving it would require data the platform does not have.

Examples of trade-offs stated rather than adjudicated:

- **Sun drying vs mechanical drying** — energy versus weather-dependence,
  contamination exposure, and land use. Not resolvable in general.
- **Concentration energy vs transport savings** — evaporating water costs energy
  but removes the weight and cold-chain burden of shipping it. Direction depends
  on the route.
- **Straw removal vs soil return** — straw sold for bedding, board, or fuel is
  straw not returned to the soil as organic matter. Stated even-handedly, with
  an explicit refusal to recommend.

## Concepts covered

Energy demand · water demand · wastewater · dust · emissions · residue streams ·
by-product utilisation · circularity · waste minimisation · heat recovery · feed
use · soil-amendment use · fuel use.

## Circularity that is real

Stated as material fact, always with a "not a claim of environmental
preferability" caveat:

- **Bagasse fuels the mill that produced it** — cane milling's residue is
  commonly burned for process energy, making the material loop close on itself.
- **Beet pulp and molasses go to feed**, so beet processing's residual streams
  are largely valorised.
- **Milling is close to fully valorised in material terms** — bran and mill feed
  to animal feed, germ to food and oil.
- **Pomace, citrus peel, and almond hulls** carry feed, pectin, seed-oil, and
  fuel uses.

## Where the real burden sits

- **Fruit and vegetable processing** — wash-water volume and wastewater organic
  load are the dominant issues, not energy.
- **Coffee wet processing** uses substantially more water than dry processing
  and produces an organically loaded effluent; dry processing trades that for
  space, time, and weather risk. Stated qualitatively, with no volumes.
- **Milling and drying** — energy and dust.
- **Solvent extraction** — vapour emissions and solvent recovery.

No figures appear anywhere in this cluster: energy, water, and emission
quantities are plant-, route-, and region-specific, and a number without that
context would be worse than none.
